First, let's talk about potatoes. Your average potato is 6 inches long and 5 inches wide. Washington Post states there are three main types of potatoes: "Starchy", "Waxy:, and "All-Purpose". Starchy potatoes like the classic Idaho or Russet, are (obviously) high in starch and low in moisture. Waxy potatoes like Red Bliss or New Potatoes, these have a low starch content and are often characterized by a creamy, firm and moist flesh that holds its shape well after cooking. All-Purpose: These potatoes have a medium starch content that fall somewhere in between the starchy and waxy potatoes.
